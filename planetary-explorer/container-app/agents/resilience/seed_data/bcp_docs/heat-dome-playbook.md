# Heat Dome Business Continuity Playbook — Texas Operations

**Owner:** Global Operations Resilience Team
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04
**Applies to:** All Texas facilities

## Trigger criteria

Activate this playbook when **all** of the following are forecast:

- Daily maximum temperature ≥ 100 °F for **3 or more consecutive days**.
- ERCOT issues a Conservation Appeal **or** Energy Emergency Alert (EEA).
- Cooling tower approach temperature is projected to exceed 8 °F above design.

## Tier-1 actions (within 24 hours of trigger)

1. **Cleanroom fab sites (Austin Fab 1, Austin Fab 2):**
   - Pre-cool process water reservoirs overnight (off-peak grid).
   - Shift non-critical maintenance to overnight shifts.
   - Defer wafer starts that would require additional cooling-tower capacity.
2. **Assembly & sub-assembly (San Antonio, Waco):**
   - Reduce ambient setpoints by 2 °F in test floors with reflow ovens.
   - Stage critical sub-assembly buffer at +1 shift to absorb upstream delay.
3. **Distribution / logistics (Dallas, Houston, Corpus, Lubbock):**
   - Move outdoor loading to 04:00–10:00 window.
   - Verify reefer container fuel reserves at Houston port docks.

## Tier-2 actions (if ERCOT EEA-2 or higher)

- Curtail Austin Fab 2 non-critical production lines by 15%.
- Shift R&D campus to remote work; close auxiliary HVAC zones.
- Activate mutual aid agreement with Phoenix backup distribution.

## Recovery / stand-down

- Stand down 24 hours after **both** ERCOT alert clears and forecast highs drop below 95 °F for 48 hours.
- Capture lessons-learned within 5 business days; update this playbook within 30 days.
