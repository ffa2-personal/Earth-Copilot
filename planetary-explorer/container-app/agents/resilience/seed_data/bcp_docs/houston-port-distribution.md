# Port of Houston Distribution — Heat & Storm Operations

**Facility scope:** `tx-dc-houston-01`, `tx-dc-corpus-01`.

## Heat operations

- Forecast highs ≥ 100 °F at the Port:
  - Stagger outdoor loading: 04:00–10:00 and 18:00–22:00 windows only.
  - Verify reefer container fuel reserves (≥ 48 hours autonomy).
  - Confirm shore-power capacity for reefer ships at berth.
- Forecast highs ≥ 103 °F for ≥ 2 days:
  - Activate cooling shelters for yard staff.
  - Limit continuous outdoor work to 30-minute rotations.

## Heat + ERCOT EEA combination

- If ERCOT issues EEA-2 with forecast highs ≥ 100 °F, reduce non-reefer
  load by 10% (lighting curtailment, non-critical HVAC zones).
- Reefer load is **never** curtailed — coordinate with utility for
  explicit reefer-protect declaration.

## Coastal storm overlay

- Heat events frequently precede tropical activity; if NHC has a
  development area within the 5-day cone, accelerate hardening:
  - Top up generator fuel.
  - Confirm marine cargo lashing kits are in inventory.

## Corpus Christi (`tx-dc-corpus-01`)

- Coastal location moderates heat (Gulf breeze) but raises hurricane risk.
- Use this Houston playbook for heat; refer to Hurricane Response — Coastal Sites for storm-specific actions (not included in this MVP).
